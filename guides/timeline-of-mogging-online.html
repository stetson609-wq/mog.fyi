Timeline primer for mogging online

A working scaffold for how mog language may have traveled across eras—each milestone needs corroboration from dated artifacts.

Without footnoted captures this outline is pedagogical—not a peer‑reviewed history.

Phase buckets (illustrative)

  1. Pre‑memeboard kernels: informal English slang & older forum irony priming binary comparison humor.
  2. Imageboard / forum diffusion: dense in‑group shitposting cross‑pollinates gymcels, looks threads—exact edges unclear.
  3. Short‑form acceleration: duet architectures, rapid caption iteration, audio meme reuse.
  4. Product codification:arenas & ladders naming mog metaphors openly (product note).
  5. Meta‑analysis memes: creators critique mog cycles while still using mog vocabulary—recursive irony cages.

How to firm a date

  • Archive.org snapshots of static pages.
  • Platform native timestamps + permalinks.
  • Cross‑posted mirrors (YouTube reuploads) with caution about edits.

Failure modes

Backdated thumbnails, edited descriptions, de‑indexed originals—all spoil naive timelines.
